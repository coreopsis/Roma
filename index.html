Rebirth

Renaissance, French re- again + naissance birth | Rinascimento, Italian ri- again + nascere be born

The Renaissance was a period of great cultural change and achievement in Europe following the Middle Ages. The era is best known for the renewed interest in the culture of classical (Greek and Roman) antiquity.

Beginning in Italy, and spreading to the rest of Europe by the 16th century, the Renaissance affected literature, philosophy, art, politics, science, religion, and other aspects of intellectual inquiry. Renaissance scholars employed the humanist method in study, and searched for realism and human emotion in art. More . . .

Site Overview

This site includes information on important artists of the Italian Resaissance as well as information on cities in which their works are located. Artist biographies are excepts from the Web Gallery of Art by Emil Kren and Daniel Marx. But refer also to a primary source on these artists: Giorgio Vasari's Lives of the Artists. Links to Vasari's Lives can be found at the bottom of each artist's page.

Information on city pages comes from Wikitravel and Wikipedia and includes links to Great Buildings Online and The New York Times where applicable.
